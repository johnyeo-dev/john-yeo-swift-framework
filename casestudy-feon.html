Where She Started

The Situation Before SWIFT

Teacher Feon had been running 1-to-1 tuition for years. She tried transitioning to group classes but found her income dipped — she couldn't sustain enough students, and the referral-only model had a low ceiling.

She had fewer than 10 students in her group classes and no system for filling them consistently. She wasn't sure how to position herself, run a webinar, or follow up with parents after an event.

🪜

The 12 Steps She Took

1

Clarified Her Why

She wrote out why she started teaching and what transformation she really gives students — not just "better grades" but confidence and clarity in learning.

2

Built Her Signature Programme Structure

She packaged her teaching into a clear 8-week group programme with defined outcomes, instead of open-ended tuition sessions.

3

Collected Testimonials First

Before launching, she reached out to past parents for written and voice testimonials. She got 6 testimonials — 3 in text, 3 in voice notes — that she used throughout the launch.

4

Set Up a Simple Webinar

She ran a free 60-minute parent webinar titled "How to Help Your Child Enjoy Learning (Not Just Exam-Drill)" on Zoom. No fancy slides — just a Google Doc script and her webcam.

5

Ran $180 of Targeted Facebook Ads

She boosted a simple post to parents in her area targeting primary school age children. She spent $180 over 10 days and got 41 registrations.

6

Used the Permission-Share Method in the Webinar

At the 45-minute mark, she asked: "I have something that might help — can I share it?" Then presented her programme naturally as the next step, not a hard sell.

7

Sent the 10-Step WhatsApp Follow-Up Sequence

She messaged every attendee individually over the next 5 days. She didn't blast a group — she sent personal DMs following the SWIFT follow-up sequence.

8

Handled Objections With the "3 Concerns" Framework

When parents said "I'll think about it" or "maybe next round," she used the 3-concern reframe: time, cost, and trust — addressing each one directly and gently.

9

Offered a 48-Hour Early Bird Slot

She created a soft deadline: the first 5 slots had an early bird price. This moved 4 parents off the fence within the first 2 days after the webinar.

10

Used Referrals to Fill the Last Spots

Once she had 10 confirmed students, she messaged them: "I have 3 spots left — if you know anyone, here's a referral discount for them." She filled 4 more spots this way.

11

Ran the Programme and Collected Ongoing Proof

She documented student wins throughout: photos of worksheets, voice notes from parents mid-programme, short video updates. This became her next launch's testimonial bank.

12

Planned Round 2 Before Round 1 Ended

In Week 6 of the programme, she announced Round 2 to current parents with a priority re-enrolment window. 9 of 17 students re-enrolled before she even ran another webinar.

🔁

What She'd Do Differently

01

Start collecting testimonials earlier. She waited until right before the launch. In hindsight, she had years of past students she could have reached out to months before.

02

Run the webinar twice. She only did one webinar date. Many parents said they wanted to attend but couldn't make the time. A second date would have doubled registrations.

03

Set up the WhatsApp broadcast list first. She manually typed individual messages for the first 2 days before creating a broadcast. That would have saved 3–4 hours.

04

Price with more confidence. She priced slightly below what she'd intended. After seeing the demand, she realised she could have priced 20% higher and still filled the class.
