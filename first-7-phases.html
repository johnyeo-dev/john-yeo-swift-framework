🧠 Before You Begin

The Mindset That Makes Everything Work

Tactics without the right headspace will burn you out. Ground yourself in these first — then every step in this roadmap will feel lighter.

🤝
Client First. Always Add Value.

Every post, every DM, every webinar — lead with what's useful for them. When parents feel genuinely helped, trust builds naturally. Sales follow trust.

🔄
If Leads Come — Great. If Not — Try Again.

No result is failure — it's data. If something doesn't pull leads, tweak the hook, change the angle, post again. You only lose when you stop.

⚡
Fail Forward Fast.

Don't spend two weeks perfecting a post. Ship it in two hours, see what lands, improve the next one. Speed of learning beats perfection every time.

🌿
Stay Calm & Relaxed.

Desperation repels. When you're grounded, your content feels safe and parents sense it. Take care of yourself — a calm tutor attracts calm, committed families.

✨
Attract Aligned, Higher-Quality Clients.

You don't need everyone. You need the right parents — the ones who value what you do and show up consistently. Be specific about who you're for. The rest will self-select.

🌱
Trust the Compound Effect.

One post won't change your business. 100 posts will. Each reel, carousel, and DM reply is a small brick — invisible alone, but over months they build something your competitors can't copy.

Phase 1

Brand Foundation

Do this once. Get it right. Everything else builds on it — your colours, voice, and niche define every piece of content you create.

1a

Define your niche

Who exactly do you help? Subject, level, and most importantly — the specific struggle. The more precise you are, the more parents feel like you're talking directly to them.

💡Bad: "I teach Maths." Good: "I help P5–P6 students stop second-guessing themselves in Paper 2."
✦ Claude Prompt
Help me define my teaching niche. I teach [subject] to [age group / level]. My students most commonly struggle with [list 2-3 specific problems]. Help me write a one-sentence niche statement in this format: "I help [who] [achieve what] by [how]." Give me 3 versions to choose from.
1b

Build your brand colour palette

Consistent colours = instant recognition. Think Tiffany's turquoise, Uniqlo's red. Upload a screenshot of your IG or logo to Claude and extract your palette.

📹 Watch First
✦ Claude Prompt — paste your screenshot after this
I'm going to paste a screenshot of my brand / social media profile / marketing materials. Please: 1. Read the colours you see from the image 2. Identify my primary colours (background, headings, key accent) 3. Identify my secondary colours (supporting tones) 4. Give me the HEX code for each colour 5. Advise me on which secondary colours to remove so the palette stays cohesive 6. Show me a visual swatch card of the final colour palette
💡Stick to 2–3 main colours. Save your HEX codes — you'll use them in every step that follows.
1c

Define your brand voice

How do you sound? Warm? Direct? Encouraging? Playful? Your voice should feel like you — so parents feel they know you before they ever meet you.

✦ Claude Prompt
Based on these 3 captions I've written [paste 3 examples], describe my brand voice in 5 words. Then write a one-paragraph tone guide I can give Claude every time I ask it to write content for me — so it always sounds like me.

Phase 2

Instagram Setup

Your storefront. First impressions happen here. A parent visits your profile in 3 seconds — your bio, pinned post, and highlights decide if they stay.

2a

Optimise your IG bio

Clear niche + who you help + one CTA + your link. Under 150 characters. A parent should know within 3 seconds if you're right for their child.

✦ Claude Prompt
Write 3 versions of my Instagram bio. I'm a [subject] tutor helping [who — e.g. P5–P6 students struggling with Paper 2]. My link is [URL]. Keep each under 150 characters. Include one emoji. End with a soft CTA like "Free guide ↓" or "DM STUDY for tips".
2b

Build your link-in-bio page

One Vercel page that houses all your lead magnets — so one bio link goes everywhere. No Linktree needed. Claude builds it in minutes.

✦ Claude Prompt
Build a mobile-first link-in-bio page for a [subject] tutor. My name is [name]. Include buttons linking to: 1. [Lead Magnet 1 title] → [URL] 2. [Lead Magnet 2 title] → [URL] 3. WhatsApp → https://wa.me/[number] 4. Instagram → https://instagram.com/[handle] Use my brand colours: primary [HEX], accent [HEX]. Font: DM Sans. Clean, modern design. Deploy on Vercel.

Create branded IG highlight covers

5 covers: Free Resources · Testimonials · About Me · Tips · Enrol Now. Consistent style = professional profile at a glance.

✦ ChatGPT Image Prompt
Create 5 Instagram highlight cover icons in flat design style. Background colour: [your HEX]. Icon colour: white. Simple, minimal icons for these 5 labels: Free Resources, Testimonials, About Me, Tips, Enrol Now. Square format, 1:1 ratio. No text — icon only. Consistent visual style across all 5.

Phase 3

Lead Magnets

Give first. Trust follows. A single web page — not a PDF — that solves one problem so well that parents immediately think: "This person really knows what they're doing."

3a

Build Lead Magnet 1 — General guide

Broad topic, wide appeal. 5–7 actionable steps on a single Vercel page. This is what you send in the ManyChat auto-DM.

General Lead Magnet
7 Proven Steps
7-proven-steps.vercel.app
✦ Claude Prompt
Build a single-page lead magnet website for a [subject] tutor. Title: "7 Proven Steps to Help Your Child [Specific Outcome]" Audience: Parents of [level] students in Singapore For each of the 7 steps: - Bold headline (the step name) - 2–3 sentences of practical advice - One thing the parent can try today End with a soft CTA: "Want personalised help? Message me on Instagram @[handle]" Design: Clean white background, [primary HEX] accent, DM Sans font, mobile-friendly. Deploy on Vercel. No login required.
3b

Build Lead Magnet 2 — Specific tool

Solves ONE exact concept interactively. A worked example, a visual, or a mini calculator. This is what you promise in the WhatsApp follow-up DM.

3c

Add email capture to both pages

Don't lose leads who browse but don't WhatsApp. A simple Google Form embedded at the bottom catches them — free, instant, no setup needed.

Wrap it in a section with a heading and short subtext. Mobile-friendly.

Phase 4

ManyChat Automation

Set it up once. It runs 24/7. When someone comments on your post, they instantly get your lead magnet — no manual sending, no missed leads.

4a

Set up your ManyChat free account

Go to ManyChat, create a free account, and connect your Instagram. The free plan is enough to run your entire funnel at the start.

🤖 Go to ManyChat (Free) ↗
💡Free plan allows: Comment-to-DM automations, keyword triggers, basic flows. Upgrade only when you're sending 1,000+ DMs/month.
4b

Create your Comment-to-DM flow

In ManyChat: New Flow → Comment trigger → choose your pinned post → set keyword (e.g. GUIDE) → auto-DM sends your lead magnet link instantly.

✦ Auto-DM Template (copy into ManyChat)
Hey [First Name]! 👋 Here's your free guide: [paste your lead magnet URL] Hope it helps! Quick question — is [specific struggle, e.g. "Paper 2 problem sums"] something your child is dealing with right now?
4c

Write your follow-up DM (30–60 min later)

After they receive the lead magnet, send a warm check-in. Promise Lead Magnet 2 via WhatsApp — and ask for their number. This is how you bridge IG DM → WhatsApp.

✦ Follow-up DM Template
Hey [First Name]! Checking in — hope the guide was helpful 😊 I actually have another free resource on [related topic] that goes a bit deeper on [specific concept]. It's easier to send it over WhatsApp. Would that be okay? What's your number? I'll drop it there for you 📲
✦ Claude Prompt — customise this for your subject
Write a follow-up DM for a [subject] tutor to send 1 hour after delivering a free guide on [topic]. The goal is to start a genuine conversation and offer a second free resource via WhatsApp to get their phone number. Warm tone, not salesy. Under 80 words.
4d

Create a second keyword flow for Lead Magnet 2

A different post, different keyword, same funnel logic. Now you have two entry points — more chances to capture different parents at different stages.

💡Use different keywords per lead magnet (e.g. GUIDE, TIPS, FREE, VISUAL) so ManyChat can track which one is working best.

Phase 5

Conversation & Closing

This is where students are won or lost. Not because of your programme — because of how you make parents feel heard. Listen first. Invite only when it's genuinely right.

5a

Set up WhatsApp Business

Separate from personal. Add your business name, description, and hours. Set up quick replies for common questions. This is where your real conversations happen.

✦ Claude Prompt — Quick Reply Templates
Write 5 WhatsApp Business quick reply templates for a [subject] tutor. Include replies for: 1. New enquiry greeting 2. "What are your rates?" 3. "Is there a trial class?" 4. "Can you send more info?" 5. Away message (outside business hours) Warm, professional, under 50 words each.
5b

Write your WhatsApp conversation script

3 questions to ask. How to listen. When to offer. The goal is understanding — not selling. Parents who feel heard trust you.

✦ Claude Prompt
Write a WhatsApp conversation script for a [subject] tutor speaking to a parent who just received a free lead magnet. Goal: understand their child's challenge, build trust, and invite them to a trial class only if it's a genuine fit. Include: - Opening message after sending Lead Magnet 2 - 3 discovery questions to ask (one at a time) - How to respond if the child's problem matches what you teach - How to respond if it's not a good fit (refer gracefully) Tone: warm, conversational, never pushy.
5c

Write your closing invitation

Not a pitch. An honest offer for the right fit. After listening, you share what you do simply — and invite them only if you genuinely believe you can help.

✦ Claude Prompt
Write 3 versions of a soft closing message a [subject] tutor can send on WhatsApp after a genuine conversation, inviting the parent to a trial class. Each version should: - Acknowledge what the parent shared - Connect it to what the tutor offers - Make a low-pressure invitation - Be under 60 words Tone: honest, warm, never salesy.
5d

Handle the common objections

"Let me think about it." "It's too expensive." "We're already with another tutor." Prepare kind, honest responses — not rebuttals.

✦ Claude Prompt
Write kind, honest WhatsApp responses to these 5 common objections a tutor hears from parents: 1. "Let me think about it." 2. "It's a bit expensive." 3. "We're already with another tutor." 4. "Can you lower the price?" 5. "I need to check with my spouse." Each response should: acknowledge the concern, not argue, and keep the door open. Under 50 words each.

Phase 6

Programme Page

Your flagship offer. This is where warm leads become paying students. Build it once — it works for you every time someone asks "what do you offer?"

6a

Define your signature programme

Name, duration, outcomes, price, who it's for. Get this clear before building the page — the page is only as strong as the offer behind it.

✦ Claude Prompt
Help me name and structure a signature [subject] programme for [target student — e.g. P6 students preparing for PSLE]. Duration: [X weeks / X sessions] Price: $[X] Help me: 1. Write a compelling programme name (give me 5 options) 2. Define the 3 key outcomes parents can expect 3. Write a one-paragraph programme description 4. Suggest what to include in each session/week
6b

Collect 3 testimonials

Ask your current or past students/parents. One WhatsApp message is all it takes. Real words from real people outperform any copy you write.

✦ Claude Prompt — testimonial request message
Write a short WhatsApp message I can send to a past student or parent asking for a testimonial. Make it easy — give them exactly 2 questions to answer: 1. What was the challenge before? 2. What changed after? Keep the message casual, genuine, and under 60 words. I'll send this to 3 people.
6c

Build your programme sales page

Hero · Problem · What's included · Testimonials · Pricing · FAQ · CTA. One page that answers every question a parent has before they message you.

Set up payment collection

ThriveCart is recommended for clean checkout pages. To start, PayNow or bank transfer with a simple confirmation message works fine.

6e

Build a simple onboarding page

What happens after they pay. First steps, what to prepare, schedule, and how to contact you. Removes anxiety. Builds confidence.

✦ Claude Prompt
Build a simple onboarding page for new students of my [programme name]. Sections: 1. Welcome message (warm, personal, reassuring) 2. What to prepare before the first session 3. Schedule: [session days/times] 4. What to expect in the first 2 weeks 5. How to contact me: [WhatsApp / email] Design: Clean, warm, friendly — use colours [HEX1] and [HEX2]. Mobile-friendly. Deploy on Vercel.

Phase 7

Social Proof & Growth

Make your results visible. Let your students' wins do the selling. This phase turns happy parents into your most powerful marketing channel.

7a

Build a testimonials / results page

Screenshots, quotes, before-and-after scores. One page that consolidates all your social proof. Link to it from your programme page and IG bio.

✦ Claude Prompt
Build a testimonials page for a [subject] tutor. Include: 1. A hero section: "Real Students. Real Results." with a subline about your niche 2. A 2-column grid of quote cards (I'll provide the testimonials) 3. A "Featured Result" section highlighting one standout student story 4. A CTA at the bottom: "Ready to see results like these? [WhatsApp / register link]" Design: trustworthy, clean — colours [HEX1] and [HEX2]. Mobile-friendly. Deploy on Vercel.
7b

Create a content calendar

3 posts/week: 1 carousel (pain point) + 1 short tip reel + 1 personal/story post. Consistency compounds — each post seeds the next lead magnet request.

🎬 For reels, use CapCut or the built-in Edit app inside Instagram — both are free and beginner-friendly.

✦ Claude Prompt
Create a 4-week Instagram content calendar for a [subject] tutor targeting [audience — e.g. parents of P5–P6 students in Singapore]. Posting rhythm: 3 posts/week - Monday: Carousel (pain point + lead magnet CTA) - Wednesday: Short tip (reel or single image) - Friday: Personal or story post (build connection) For each post, give me: - Hook idea (first line) - Content angle - CTA suggestion Focus topics: [list 3–4 recurring themes relevant to your subject]
7c

Build a referral system

Your happiest parents are your best marketers. Make it easy and natural for them to refer a friend. One WhatsApp message is all it takes to start.

✦ Claude Prompt
Write a referral WhatsApp message I can send to current parents asking them to recommend my [subject] class to one friend whose child is struggling. Include: - A genuine reason to reach out (not transactional) - A simple ask: "If you know anyone who could benefit…" - Optional: a thank-you offer (e.g. one free session, discount on next month) Keep it real, under 80 words, not like a marketing email.
7d

Create a case study page

One student story. Before → struggle → what you did together → transformation. The most powerful sales page you'll ever have — because it's true.

✦ Claude Prompt
Build a case study page for my [subject] tutoring. Student: [first name only, or "a student" for privacy]. Sections: 1. Hero: "[Student Name]'s Story" — one-line outcome 2. The Challenge: what they were struggling with before 3. What We Did Together: your approach (3–4 sentences) 4. The Result: specific improvement (score, confidence, mindset) 5. In Their Words: one parent quote 6. CTA: "Is this your child's story too? [WhatsApp link]" Design: emotional, clean — colours [HEX1] and [HEX2]. Deploy on Vercel.
