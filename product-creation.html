Product Strategy · Full Stack Edition

⚡ Product Creation — From Idea to Stack

From idea to product stack without creating anything first.

Don't create the product first and hope there's demand. Run the session first. Sell the seats. The session IS the product creation. Everything after is packaging.
🪜

Stage 1
🎯

Validate Demand Through a Live Session

1

Pick the Product Idea Based on Your Best Client's Need

Don't ask "is there demand?" — ask "what does she keep asking me about?" That is your demand signal.

2

Sell Tickets to a Live Zoom Session Before You Create Anything

Price: $20–$50 per seat. Frame it as:

"Join me for a live [topic] session. First group gets the recording and a PDF summary included — all for the early bird price."

You earn while you create. If nobody buys the ticket — the demand isn't there yet. No wasted effort.

3

Run the Session with Fathom Recording + Zoom Cloud Recording Active

  • Fathom captures the transcript (for converting to PDF/text products)
  • Zoom captures the video (for video add-ons and future waves)
  • Run it like you're teaching your best student — not performing for a camera
Stage 2
🌊

Wave 1 Buyers — Founding Members

4

Give Wave 1 Buyers the Full Live Experience + Raw Recordings for Free

They paid the early price. In return they get:

  • Live attendance
  • Zoom session recording (unedited is fine for Wave 1)
  • PDF summary from the transcript (Claude converts this for you)
  • Your direct attention during the live Q&A

This is your highest-trust group. They become your testimonial sources and your best referrers.

5

Collect Feedback from Wave 1 Before Editing Anything

Google Form or WhatsApp voice note. Ask:

  • What was most useful?
  • What was confusing?
  • What would you add?

This shapes your final product before you invest editing time.

6

Edit the Zoom Recording in Descript

Descript lets you edit video by editing the transcript text — delete a word in the transcript, the video clip is cut. No timeline editing needed.

Cut: long silences, technical glitches, off-topic tangents, screen-share setup moments.

Keep: your actual teaching, student Q&A (these are gold), moments of insight that landed well.

7

Add Value Layers in Descript

  • Add text captions (auto-generated, then clean up)
  • Add intro/outro slides
  • Add chapter markers so buyers can navigate by topic
  • Export as MP4
8

Create a Short Highlight Reel (2–3 Minutes) from the Full Recording

Use this as your marketing asset for Wave 2. Post as a Reel, TikTok, or YouTube Short. This is your social proof + sales tool in one.

Stage 4
📝

Convert Transcript to Text Products (Claude)

9

Export Fathom Transcript → Paste into Claude

Prompts to use:

"Convert this session transcript into a clean revision notes PDF for [level] students. Use clear headers, simple language, worked examples where possible."
"Turn this into a structured workbook — include explanations, practice questions, and a self-assessment at the end."
"Create a one-page parent summary of what was covered in this session and what to practise at home."
10

Design the Cover and Layout in Canva

  • Use your brand colours
  • Add your photo (credibility)
  • Label it "Volume 1" even if Volume 2 doesn't exist yet
  • Export as PDF
Stage 5
🎬

Build the Video Add-On Product

Host the Video for Wave 2 Buyers

  • Gumroad — simple, pay once, get link
  • Google Drive — unlisted folder, share link after payment
  • Teachable / Kajabi — if you want a proper course platform
  • Loom — simple hosting, shareable link
13

Wave 2 Buyers Get the Edited Video — Not the Live Session

"Wave 1 joined live and got the raw recording. Wave 2 gets the edited, cleaned-up version — chapter-marked and captioned."

This makes Wave 2 feel like they're getting a polished product, not leftovers. Both groups feel they got a good deal.

Stage 6
📦

Stage 8
🤝

The Meet Your Book Buyer Session

16

After Every Product Launch, Run a Buyer-Exclusive Zoom Session

Free for buyers. Invite-only. Topics:

  • Walk through how to use the product
  • Answer their top 3 questions live
  • Add bonus tips not in the book
  • Naturally introduce your next level (workshop, programme)

This converts product buyers into lesson/programme clients. They already trust you — they bought your book. The session closes the gap.

📅

The Full 4-Week Timeline

Week 1
→Identify topic (ask your best client)
→Open Wave 1 registrations ($20–$50/seat)
→Run live Zoom session (Fathom + Zoom recording)
Week 2
→Export Fathom transcript → Claude → PDF draft
→Design cover in Canva
→Deliver PDF + raw Zoom recording to Wave 1 buyers
→Collect Wave 1 feedback via Google Form
Week 3
→Edit video in Descript (cut, caption, chapter-mark)
→Refine PDF based on feedback
→Create highlight reel for social media
→Launch Wave 2 ($40–$100 for PDF + edited video)
Week 4
→Host "Meet Your Book Buyer" session
→Upsell into workshop or programme
→Decide if demand justifies print run
→Repurpose content across all channels
