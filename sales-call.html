Sales Framework · 7 Stages

📞 The Sales Call Framework — For Tutors & Educators

Based on Jamie's High-Ticket Closing Masterclass. A heart-based approach to identifying mental blocks and guiding parents toward a decision — without pressure.

The Mindset Shift Before Anything Else
"Sales is not about making a transaction. It's about identifying the mental blocks stopping someone from moving towards what they already want — and helping them see through those blocks using their own logic."
— Jamie
🔍

Diagnostic work — helping a student identify where they're stuck

🔄

Reframing work — turning a wrong method into the right one

🎯

Closing work — guiding a parent to see their child's potential

The only difference in a sales call: there's a transaction at the end.

1

Set the Frame

First 2–5 minutes · You lead, not them
Why it matters: If you lose the frame here, the parent will lead the call — and you'll end up on a 2-hour emotional unloading session with no sale.
"Hi [Name], can you see me okay? Great. I'm John. So I saw that you reached out about support for [child's name] — I'd love to hear more about where things are at. Can you give me a quick 30-second intro of who you are?"
"Okay great. So here's what we'll do today — I'll ask you a few questions to understand [child's name]'s situation, and then I'll share a bit about how I work. If there's a fit, I'll walk you through how we can work together. And if there isn't, no worries — I'll point you in the right direction either way. Sound good?"
Keep their intro to 30 seconds — don't let them ramble
State the agenda so they follow your flow, not their own
Project calm confidence — not enthusiasm, not eagerness
2

The Diagnostic

20 minutes · They reveal their pain
Why it matters: People only believe what they say themselves. If you tell them their child needs help, they push back. If they say it themselves, they believe it deeply.
Layer 1 · Current Situation

"So tell me, how is [child's name] doing in [subject] right now?"

Layer 2 · The Emotion Under the Facts

"How do you feel about that?" → If flat answer: "What does that mean for you? What does 'like that' actually look like at home?"

Layer 3 · The Future Vision

"If we could fast forward — what would it look like if [child's name] was doing well? How would that change things for him and for you?"

Layer 4 · Widen the Gap

"What if nothing changes from here? What does that cost you — and him?"

Don't give advice yet — you're listening, not coaching
Attach facts to emotions, not just outcomes
Anchor these answers — you'll use them in your pitch
3

Pre-empt Objections

10 minutes · Close doors before price
Why it matters: Once price is said, tension builds and trust drops. Handle objections before you reveal the number.
Pre-empt: Priority
"On a scale of 1–10, how important is it to solve this before [PSLE / exam / new school year]?"
If 7 or below: "What would make it a 10? What's holding it back from being more urgent?"
Pre-empt: Decision-Making
"When you find something that feels right for [child's name], are you someone who can make a decision on the same day, or do you usually need more time?"
Pre-empt: Investment Mindset
"Have you invested in support like this before? How did you think about that decision?"
4

The Transition

2–3 minutes · Bridge to your solution
Why it matters: Don't jump straight into your pitch — earn the right to present it. Mirror back what they said so they feel heard.
"Okay, so just to make sure I've understood — [child's name] is currently at C grade, it's affecting his confidence, and you've tried a few things but nothing has stuck. And for you, it's important that he improves before PSLE because you want him to get into [school]. Is that right?"

Wait for them to say yes. Then:

"Based on what you've shared, I think I can help. Let me walk you through how I work — and you can tell me if it feels like a fit."
5

The Pitch

5–10 minutes · Outcomes, not features
❌ Don't say

"You get 12 lessons over 3 months."

✅ Say instead

"Your child gets a full roadmap from now to PSLE — every key topic, progress checks every term, holiday workshops included."

"You mentioned that [child's name] is struggling with confidence. In the first month, that's exactly what we focus on — building the foundation so he stops second-guessing himself. By Month 3, we're drilling exam technique. By the time PSLE comes, he'll be walking in prepared — not just hoping for the best."
The what vs. the how: Share what results they'll get. Keep how you teach for when they're enrolled — that's what they're paying for.
"The full programme is $X for the year, which includes everything I've just walked you through. There's also a term-by-term option if that works better for your cashflow."

Then go quiet. Let them respond.
6

Handle Objections

As long as needed · Agree → Isolate → Tackle → Re-close
"It's expensive."
"Yeah, I totally understand — it is a significant investment."

Then isolate: "When you say you need to think about it — what are the one or two things you're most unsure about?"

If value issue: "If [child's name] does well in PSLE and gets into his dream school, what's that worth to you? And what's it costing right now to stay where things are?"

If cash flow: "I do have a term payment option — you'd pay $X every 3 months instead of upfront. Would that work better?"
"I need to discuss with my husband/wife."
"Of course, that makes sense. Can I ask — if your husband/wife is fully on board, would you want to move forward?"

If yes: "Great — would it help to hop on a quick call together? I'm happy to walk both of you through it."
"Let me think about it."
"Totally. Just so I understand — what would make you feel more confident about moving forward? Is it more information, or is it more time?"
The re-close: After handling every objection, always bring it back: "So if those two things are sorted — would you want to go ahead?"
7

The Close

Final 2–3 minutes · Clear yes or no
"Based on everything we've talked about, it really does sound like this is what [child's name] needs right now. Shall we get started?"
"I really enjoyed this conversation and I can see myself working with [child's name]. I want to be honest — I do think this is the right fit. But it's your decision. What would you like to do?"
If yes: Confirm start date → send payment link same day → follow up within 24 hours with onboarding details.

If no: "No worries at all. Let me share a few resources that might still be helpful. And if things change, feel free to reach out." Leave the door open. Never pressure. Never chase.
