Partnerships & Your Dream 100

How heart-centred tutors build win-win collaborations that grow their reach without running ads.

The Concept

What is Your Dream 100?

Your Dream 100 is a curated list of people and communities who already have the trust of your ideal clients — Singapore parents and students.

Instead of building an audience from scratch, you partner with them to reach families who are actively looking for educational support. Every parent in your Dream 100's community has already raised their hand and said: "I care deeply about my child's education." That's your people. You just need a bridge.

💛
It's not WHAT you know — it's WHO you know. And your network is your net worth.
Your Target List — Who Is In Your Dream 100?
Parent community leaders
Facebook group admins, school PSG leaders, parent bloggers & Telegram channel owners
Complementary educators
Piano, art, coding, reading teachers — same families, different subjects
Education content creators
PSLE TikTokers, parenting YouTubers, Instagram study-tips pages
Schools & enrichment centres
MOE teachers, counsellors, enrichment brands in non-competing subjects
Local parent networks
Neighbourhood groups, grassroots communities, baby playgroups (future PSLE parents!)
Fellow tutors & coaches
More established tutors, education entrepreneurs with overlapping audiences
Discovery — Where to Find Your Dream 100
Which Facebook groups do Singapore parents hang out in?
Who are the school counsellors or form teachers parents trust?
Who are the PSLE or parenting content creators on Instagram & TikTok?
Which parenting blogs or Telegram newsletters do families in SG read?
Which YouTube channels do parents watch for study tips?
Which education events (school fairs, parent talks) gather your ideal families?
Which local enrichment brands are visible but serve different subjects?
Which tutors do you already know who serve the same families?
Instagram — Build Your Dream 100 on IG
Finding them on Instagram
  • Search hashtags: #PSLEtips #SGparents #SingaporeMom #tutor #primaryschool #mathtutor
  • Explore tab: Instagram shows accounts similar to those you follow — notice who has an engaged parent audience
  • Look at your followers: which parent community leaders or educators are already following you?
  • Check who comments: on education accounts in Singapore — active commenters are warm prospects
How to engage & warm them up
  • Follow + leave genuine comments: go beyond 'great post!' — reference something specific they shared
  • Reply to their Stories: one of the fastest ways to start a real conversation in the DMs
  • Share their content to your Stories: with a genuine caption — they'll notice and often reshare back
  • Send a warm DM after engaging: once you've commented a few times, a short personal message feels natural, not cold
Instagram DM Script — Sample Message to a Dream 100 Partner
Instagram DM Script
Use after you've engaged with their content at least 2–3 times. Keep it warm, specific, and low-pressure.
"Hey [Name]! I've been following your content for a little while now and I genuinely love the way you [specific thing they do well — e.g. make PSLE prep feel less overwhelming for parents]. Your post on [specific topic] really resonated with me.

I'm John — I help P5 and P6 students with Maths and I run a community of parents working through the PSLE journey.

I'd love to feature you in a short conversation for my community — I think your perspective on [topic] would be so valuable for the parents I work with. Would you be open to a quick 20-minute chat? No prep needed — just a real conversation. 😊"
Be specific — generic DMs get ignored Reference their actual content One clear ask only No pressure close
The Golden Rule — Add Value Before You Ask for Anything
Dig the well before you're thirsty. Reach out to give — not to get. The ask comes much later.
1
Universal Currency — easiest to give
Genuine compliments, celebrating their wins publicly, leaving thoughtful comments, sharing their posts on your Stories. Costs nothing.
2
Professional Currency — your expertise
Referring students their way, sharing useful resources, offering to collaborate on a free session, writing a genuine testimonial.
3
Personal Currency — deepest connection
Showing up for them as a human — not just a business contact. This level creates relationships that last years.
Value You Can Give — What Tutors Can Realistically Offer
Free session for their community
Study skills, exam strategy, or a PSLE parent talk — done for free, zero sales pitch
Feature them in your content
Interview them, share their story on your feed or Stories — everyone loves genuine recognition
Refer students to them
If a family needs a subject you don't teach, send them to your Dream 100. Generosity is remembered.
Share your frameworks & resources
A useful worksheet, a tips post, a visual guide — valuable content that helps their audience too
Public testimonial
A heartfelt post or Story tagging them. Specific and genuine beats generic every time.
A personal message of appreciation
Simply tell them what their work meant to you. No agenda. This costs nothing and lands deeply.
Principles — How to Build These Relationships the Right Way
01
Don't do it only when you need something
Build connections before you ever need a favour. People sense when outreach is self-serving.
02
Make it easy for them to say yes
Clear ask, short time commitment, minimal effort on their end. Respect their bandwidth.
03
Take the initiative first
Send the DM. Leave the comment. The tutors who grow fastest are willing to go first.
04
Be consistent, not occasional
A quick comment weekly does more than one long message every six months.
05
Lead with genuine care
Your empathy as an educator is your greatest asset. Let it show — it builds trust fast.
06
Treat it like a long game
Some great partners take 6–12 months of relationship-building first. That's sustainable, not slow.
The Ask — Types of Collaboration That Work for Tutors
Wait for these signs before making the ask: they've engaged with your content, responded to your DMs, or mentioned your work to others.
Free sharing sessionRun a free talk for their parent or student community — study skills, PSLE tips, mindset.
Interview swapYou feature each other's expertise. Both audiences grow; trust transfers naturally.
Audience / list swapYou promote their free event to your list; they promote yours. Simple and effective.
Co-host a parent workshopPartner on a topic you both care about. Shared effort, shared audience, shared credibility.
Guest in their communityAppear in their WhatsApp group, Telegram channel, or Facebook group as a helpful guest.
IG Story feature swapShare each other to your respective Instagram Stories — instant warm introduction to a new audience.
Sample Scripts — How to Craft Your Collaboration Ask
Warm — someone you already know
"Hey [Name], hope things have been good on your end! 😊

I've been seeing your posts lately and the work you're doing with [their audience] is so aligned with what I care about too.

Would you be open to a quick collab? I could do a free session for your community on [topic], and I'd love to feature you in mine too.

No pressure — just thought it might be fun and useful for our parents! 😊"
Cold-warm — someone you follow online
"Hi [Name], I've been following your content for a while and just want to say — the way you [specific thing they do well] genuinely inspired me.

I'm John, a Maths tutor working with P5 and P6 families in Singapore. We're in a pretty similar world — different subjects, same mission.

I'd love to feature you in a short interview for my community. It'll be on [topic relevant to them], should only take 20–30 mins, and I'll handle everything and share it back with you too.

Would that be something you'd be open to?"
Your Next Steps — Start This Week
1
Write down 10–20 names
People you already know whose audiences overlap yours — fellow tutors, parent leaders, education creators.
2
Engage before you ask
Follow them on Instagram, Facebook, or Telegram. Leave 2–3 genuine, specific comments on their content.
3
Send 3 appreciation messages this week
No agenda. A genuine note of thanks or a useful resource. Do this before making any ask.
4
Make one warm ask
Once the relationship feels warm (they're engaging with you too), make a simple, single, low-pressure ask.
5
Keep it going monthly
One new collaboration a month compounds into a very different business 12 months from now.

Your network is your net worth.

The tutors who grow fastest aren't always the best teachers. They're the ones willing to build bridges, show up for others, and ask for help when the time is right.

Start today. One relationship at a time.
